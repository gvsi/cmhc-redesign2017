All groups are free and confidential.

Are you an international student at UT and also feeling:

  • Homesick?
  • Worried about your English speaking/writing skills?
  • Concerned about your legal status?
  • Unsure of how to make American friends?
  • Confused or frustrated about the American culture?
  • Alone and as if no one understands your experience?
  • Eager to learn about how to succeed in your classes?

If you answered YES to any of these questions, please join us for the Survival Strategies Workshop Series for International Students! This workshop is a place you can learn how to improve your life and academics. These meetings also give you an opportunity to meet other international students with similar experiences. We invite all international students to participate, and you may come to one, two, or ALL of the workshop meetings. It's up to you!

Each meeting will discuss a different topic (see below). You are also welcome to discuss any topics that are relevant to your experience as an international student even if they are not on the list.

2/10/17 Navigating the American classroom
2/17/17 Working with professors/supervisors
2/24/17 Challenges of communicating in English
3/3/17 Loneliness and homesickness
3/10/17 Cultural adjustment and culture shock
3/24/17 Maintaining existing relationships and making new friends
3/31/17 Assertive communication
4/7/17 Dealing with prejudice and discrimination


Professional staff at the Counseling and Mental Health Center who are experienced in working with International Students will be facilitating the discussions. Please contact Dr. Sylvia Chen at (512) 471-3515 if you have any questions or if you need more information.
