After Silence: A Support Group for Survivors of Familial Abuse

Wednesdays
1pm - 2:30pm

All groups are free and confidential.

After Silence is a closed, unstructured group for students who identify as survivors of abuse within their family of origin (parents, siblings, grandparents, etc.) and who are actively seeking healing around issues related to these experiences. Members may include survivors of verbal, emotional, financial, and/or physical abuse.

It is important to recognize that the healing process is unique to each individual person. This group seeks to empower members through the exploration of each member's unique experience as they connect and learn with other survivors in a supportive and safe environment. Through the incorporation of creativity and empathy, it is the goal of this group to provide members with a mutual sense of awareness and validation that this type of interpersonal violence is not their fault and that they are not alone.

In most cases, before joining a group or class, students meet with the group leaders to determine if a particular group is a good fit for them. To set up a pre-group interview, please call (512) 471-3515.
