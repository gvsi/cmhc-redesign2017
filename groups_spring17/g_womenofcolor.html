Finding Our Voice: A Women of Color Discussion Group

Mondays
12pm - 1:30pm
Meets at the Gender & Sexuality Center, SAC 2.112

All groups are free and confidential.

The Women of Color Discussion Group meets weekly to engage in safe, inviting, and authentic discussion around students' experiences as women of color on the UT campus. We invite both undergraduate and graduate women to join in the discussion. Women are welcome to discuss any and all topics that are relevant to their experiences (e.g., race relations, gender equality, media influences on self-esteem/body image, racism, sexism, career development, dating relationships, sexuality). There are no limits to what is discussed, and your privacy will be respected. You are welcome to join and bring a friend.

Please note that the Women of Color Discussion Group is intended to offer a safe space for women of ethnoracial minority identities. However, the group is open to all women of color, regardless of racial and ethnic background. To ensure that the group offers a safe space, leaders will invite the group to explore their racial, ethnic, and gender identity in the context of discussion that affirms the existence, and psychosocial impact, of racism and sexism.
