Making Peace with Yourself Through Self-Compassion

This group is closed for this semester, check back for the new schedule.

All groups are free and confidential.

Most of us are our own worst critics, but sometimes we’re particularly harsh with ourselves. This can create a number of challenges such as depression, social anxiety, and perfectionism – which ultimately can be debilitating and make it even harder to feel good about ourselves. This 4-week, structured, skills-based class is designed to help you improve your relationship with yourself by finding a healthier balance between self-kindness and self-accountability. Students learn how to treat themselves with greater compassion while still holding themselves responsible and working toward positive growth and change. In part, this is accomplished through learning mindfulness meditation techniques, which cultivate the kind of self-compassion that helps us be more self-accepting but also more successful in life. Mindfulness is the practice of paying attention in the present moment, on purpose, and in an open, nonjudgmental way.

We ask that prospective participants attend an Information Session on October 18th. The class itself lasts 4 weeks, from 10/25-11/15. To sign up or for more information, call CMHC at (512) 471-3515.
