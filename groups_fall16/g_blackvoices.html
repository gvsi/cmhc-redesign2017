Black Voices: A Supportive Group for Black Students

Tuesdays
3pm - 4:30pm
GWB 3200A

All groups are free and confidential.

Black Voices: A Supportive Group for Black Students meets weekly to engage in safe, inviting, and authentic discussion around students' experiences as Black & African American graduate and undergraduate students on the UT campus. Students are welcome to discuss any and all topics that are relevant to their experiences (e.g., race relations, sociopolitical issues, transmisogynoir, misogyny, mass incarceration, police brutality, cultural appropriation, Islamophobia, career development, spiritual identity, heteronormativity, sexuality and dating in the Black community). There are no limits to what is discussed, and your privacy will be respected. You are welcome to join and bring a friend. Black Voices is a confidential group.

Please note that Black Voices Discussion Group is intended to offer a safe space for Black and African American ethnoracial minority identities. The group is open to all Black-identified students, regardless of specific nationality or racial/ethnic background. To ensure that the group offers a safe space, leaders will invite the group to explore their racial and ethnic identity in the context of discussion that affirms the existence of and psychosocial impact of racism and all other forms of marginalization and systemic oppression.
