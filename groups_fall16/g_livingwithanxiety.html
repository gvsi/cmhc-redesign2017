Do anxious thoughts and feelings dominate your daily living experiences?
Do you lay awake at night worrying about future events or tasks you need to complete?
Do you avoid people or situations for fear you will be judged as awkward or anxious?

Living with Anxiety is an acceptance-based approach for individuals who struggle with problems related to anxiety and who want to:

  1. Learn more about anxiety and examine its impact on your life.
  2. Learn techniques to help anchor your thoughts to the present moment.
  3. Learn how you can stop reacting to unwanted thoughts, feelings, and physical sensations.
  4. Learn to focus attention and energy on what matters most in your life.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) is a behavioral and cognitive therapy guiding the Living with Anxiety group.

In most cases, before joining a group or class, students meet with the group leaders to determine if a particular group is a good fit for them. To set up a pre-group interview, please call (512) 471-3515.
